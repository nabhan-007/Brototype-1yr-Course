Hi everyone! When I heard today's theme was Kalla Kali, I knew exactly what to talk about. I played a lot of street football growing up.

I actually started as a Goalkeeper, and then finally moved up to be a Defender. But recently, I tore my ligament and destroyed my meniscus. After a surgery and a ton of physical therapy... I am officially back to being a Goalkeeper. (Pause here and smile, the crowd will definitely laugh)

So, since I am back in the goal, let me tell you about the greatest Kalla Kali in street football history: The Imaginary Crossbar.

We never had real goalposts. We just used two chappals, two big rocks, or water bottles filled with sand. That was it.

Because of this, whenever someone took a high shot above my waist or over my head, my Oscar-level acting began. I would wave my hands and confidently shout, 'Over! That went over the post!' I would argue for 10 minutes straight until the other team just got tired and gave up.

Looking back, defending an imaginary crossbar wasn't just me cheating. I was basically doing early-stage negotiation and QA testing. In the tech world, we complain when clients 'move the goalposts' on a project. But back then, I was doing it literally!

My name is Muhammed Nabhan. Thank you all for listening!
